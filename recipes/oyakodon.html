Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a Japanese rice bowl dish, composed of the two main ingredients: chicken and egg. 'Oyako' means parent and child in Japanese, referring here to both those ingredients in what I consider exactly my kind of humour. 'Don' is short for 'donburi', meaning rice bowl.

Made with onions and a teriyaki-style sauce, it's a dish both sweet, salty, and savoury. With chicken thighs and the benefit of Britain's world-class eggs, you get a very tender and rich meal with a filling bowl of rice to soak up the sauce.

Ingredients

Oyakodon

  • 1/4 Onion
  • 150g Chicken Thighs, Boneless and Skinless
  • 1 Tbsp Sake
  • 2 Large Egg
  • 1 Serving of Cooked Rice

Sauce

  • 60 ml Dashi
  • 1 Tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Mirin
  • 1 tsp Sugar

Directions

Preparation

  1. Combine the sauce ingredients together and stir until combined.
  2. Slice the onion into large strips.
  3. Trim the fat from the chicken thighs, cut into diagonal strips and mix in a bowl with 1 Tbsp sake, let rest 5 minutes.
  4. Crack the eggs into a bowl and use chopsticks to break the whites while leaving the yolks whole, once they are well broken, poke the yolks to break them but don't mix.

Cooking

  1. Add the onions and sauce to a cold pan, set to medium heat and bring to a simmer.
  2. Once simmering, add the chicken and cook until the chicken is done.
  3. Add the egg mixture into the centre of the pan, trying to get mostly the whites.
  4. When the whites are set but not dry, add the rest of the eggs to the pan.
  5. Let sit a few moments, then slide the contents of the pan onto a bowl of rice.

Notes

  • You can adjust the quantity of soy sauce depending on whether your rice is salted or not. I tend to add a little more, since I prefer my food well salted.
